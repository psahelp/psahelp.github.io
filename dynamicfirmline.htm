Dynamic Firm Line

Setting and changing the behaviour of the dynamic firm line is achieved through work centre maintenance for the relevant work centre.

For Auto-Sync users who may not be interested in the position of Firm lines which are only a planning tool they can be hidden by right clicking an open area of the planning board and selecting ‘Hide Firm Line’


Freeze Active/Freeze Moving/Isolate Firm Zone – these define ways of separating the area after the time line on any work centre into firm and free zones. With just Freeze active turned on a dragable firm line is added to the display. Tasks within the firm area are passed to SFDC – those outside are retained in PSA or withdrawn from SFDC if previously transferred. The line remains at the last point it is dragged to and will be ‘overtaken’ by the time line if not moved regularly.
Turning on ‘Freeze Moving’ has the effect that the firm line will stay the same distance in front of the time line. This prevents it being overtaken but has the effect that tasks outside the firm zone will fall inside the firm zone, and so be passed to SFDC, without any action being taken.
To overcome these limitations we have ‘Isolate Firm Zone’ where a number of hours: minutes to define the zone should be entered. Task will always be scheduled outside this firm zone. They can only get inside the firm zone if dragged on the planning board or moved on the dynamic work list. You can also adjust the size of the firm zone on the dynamic work list. The flag on the planning board will always be the defined number of available hours, ignoring non working periods, ahead of the time line. If you overload the firm zone by putting say 100 estimated hours into an 80 hour zone this will push the flag back further to keep a clear distinction between tasks inside and tasks outside the zone. There are no borderline cases – every task is fully in or fully out of the zone.
