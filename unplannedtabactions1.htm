Screen by screen descriptions > Schedule Control

Unplanned tab actions

The filter area is normally hidden – move the mouse over the green bar on the left and the filter area appears – it remains visible until the filter button is pressed leaving a cleaner screen

Orders may be filtered by work centre or display code, customer, order number like, live/tentative status and delivery date. Filter values are remembered and turned on and off by check boxes.
A separate filter button refreshes the list after changing settings

To change the column selection or sequence see the Preferences tab

The list may be sorted by any column, ascending or descending – click the column heading.
Column widths may be adjusted by dragging borders in the title area.

Right click on any order gives a menu allowing drill down to order or task detail (component, task number and work centre shown) or single click planning.
The expand/compress option will show multiple values of technical attributes so that orders ma be selected in groups if required. If technical attributes exist at header level only this will have no effect.

The Sequence button allows choice as to where newly planned orders insert into the planned order list. Choose from the top of the list, the bottom of the list, down the list based on delivery date or down the list based on calculated latest start time of the first task.

The Register remains for backward compatibility with much earlier versions. For new users it is recommended to disable it – Configuration/System/Functionality tab. The only benefit is that a group of orders can be highlighted and ‘Copy’ed to the Register or Orders can be double clicked which also adds them to the register. Once in the register you still have to open it and ‘Plan’ all the orders within it. It is usually simpler just to right click an order and select ‘Plan’ which will transfer it to the Planned tab in the location dictated by the Sequence button. Also associated with the Register is the Copy button. This allows orders satisfying delivery date or technical attribute criteria be ‘Copy’ed to the Register and then planned. However the unplanned tab can now show – and be sorted by – both the delivery date and any technical attribute.

The Tentative button is used where a ‘Live’ order with a new order number has arrived in PSA and it must replace a ‘Tentative’ placeholder order. After the new order has been selected press the tentative button. A new screen opens listing all the scheduled tentative orders. Various filters are available to reduce the list. Once the order to replace has been selected press the OK button and the new order and the tentative original go into the difference check routine as for normal replacements. The final result is the new order has the scheduled slots originally held by the tentative order that no longer exists.
